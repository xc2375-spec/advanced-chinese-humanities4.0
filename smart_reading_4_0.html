中国女性的社会地位

第四单元 · 开篇 · 女性・婚姻・社会

中国历史上歧视妇女、压迫妇女的现象很多。但并不是从一开始就是这样，妇女地位越来越低主要是从宋朝开始的。

🌏 Cultural Note — Historical Overview
📌
The text opens with a key claim: discrimination against women was not always present in Chinese history, but intensified starting with the Song Dynasty (宋朝, 960–1279 CE). This "decline narrative" is a central argument of the essay — pay attention to how it unfolds across paragraphs.
📖
越来越低 — "lower and lower; increasingly low." The pattern 越来越 + adjective means "more and more ___." You'll see it throughout the text (e.g., 越来越多, 越来越高). It signals a progressive change over time.

宋朝以前，中国的妇女还是非常开放的。比方说，汉朝末年的蔡文姬虽然已经在匈奴结婚，而且生了两个孩子，但回到汉朝的土地以后，她又一次出嫁。唐朝的公主嫁过三次的有三位，嫁过两次的有二十七位。可以说，宋朝以前，再嫁并不是什么见不得人的事，社会不要求女人从一而终，对贞女也不太在乎。唐朝以前，妇女不但有再嫁的自由，而且可以参加各种各样的公共活动。

🌏 Cultural Note — Pre-Song Openness
👩‍🎨
蔡文姬 (Cài Wénjī) — A celebrated poet and musician of the late Han Dynasty (c. 177–249 CE). She was captured by the Xiongnu (匈奴), a nomadic steppe people, married a Xiongnu chieftain, and had children with him. She was later ransomed by the Han general Cao Cao and remarried. Her story, especially her lament poem 胡笳十八拍, is famous in Chinese literary history.
🏯
The Tang Dynasty (唐朝, 618–907 CE) is often considered a relatively cosmopolitan and socially open era in Chinese history. Foreign cultures, religions (Buddhism, Islam, Nestorianism), and fashions were embraced. The high remarriage rate among Tang princesses reflects a less rigid social code than later periods.
📜
从一而终 (cóng yī ér zhōng) — literally "follow one [husband] to the end [of life]." This Confucian ideal, demanding a woman's lifelong loyalty to one husband (including not remarrying after widowhood), became a strict social expectation only after the Song Dynasty strengthened Neo-Confucian orthodoxy.

你也许听说过中国妇女有缠足的习惯，其实这种现象是从宋朝才开始流行起来的，此后中国女性的地位变得越来越低。在宋朝和以后的几百年中，女孩子如果不缠足，有一双大脚，就会被人耻笑，甚至找不到婆家。有一双小脚的女孩子才被认为是美的，所以在女孩还很小的时候，大人就开始给她们缠足，不让脚自然地生长。缠足以后，就像穿上了高跟鞋一样，完全改变了女孩子走路的样子。女孩子再也不能出去参加各种活动了，她们被禁锢在家里，无法了解、关心外面的世界，成了男性的奴隶。

🌏 Cultural Note — Foot-Binding (缠足)
🦶
Foot-binding (缠足) began in the Song Dynasty and persisted until the early 20th century. Girls' feet were tightly bound from age 4–6 to prevent growth, aiming for a "golden lotus" (金莲) of about 3 inches. The practice caused permanent bone deformation and was intensely painful but was considered a mark of beauty and femininity — and was essentially required for marriage into respectable families.
🏛️
The practice was connected to Neo-Confucianism's increasingly strict gender norms, which emphasized female modesty, confinement to the home, and submission. Foot-binding physically enforced this confinement — a woman with bound feet could not walk far or fast. Anti-foot-binding campaigns began in the late Qing Dynasty and were accelerated by the May Fourth Movement (1919).
📝 Vocabulary Note — 甚至 vs. 即使 / 再也不能
🔎
甚至 (shènzhì) = "even; so much so that" — used to introduce an extreme, surprising, or additional consequence. Example: 她不但找不到工作，甚至连家都没了。 (She not only couldn't find work, she even lost her home.) Watch for 甚至 and 即使 appearing in the same text — they serve very different functions.
🔎
再也不能…了 — "can never again…; no longer able to." The adverb 再也 + negative verb emphasizes a permanent, irreversible inability. Compare: 不能 (can't right now) vs. 再也不能 (can never again). Example: 缠足以后，她再也不能自由地奔跑了。

1919年5月4日，中国发生了著名的"五四"运动。这以后，缠足现象才慢慢消失。"五四"运动给中国社会带来了很多新的风气，但家庭中的夫权观念、女性必须"三从四德"的文化延续了下来，几十年中一直没有太大的变化。做妻子的要听丈夫的话，做女儿的要听父母的话，做媳妇的要听公婆的话，许多女性的家庭权益受到侵犯。更糟糕的是，家庭中侵犯女性权益的人常常也是女性——如婆婆、后母。"多年的媳妇熬成婆"，婆婆们经常用一种不健康的心态，重复自己的婆婆当年用过的办法，有时候甚至更严重地侵犯媳妇的权益和自由。后母虐待女儿的情况也经常发生。即使在家中没有受到婆婆或后母的虐待，女性也得受到父亲和公公的压迫。

🌏 Cultural Note — May Fourth Movement & 三从四德
✊
The May Fourth Movement (五四运动, 1919) — On May 4, 1919, students in Beijing protested the Treaty of Versailles, which awarded Germany's former territories in China to Japan. The movement quickly became a broad cultural and intellectual revolution calling for science (科学), democracy (民主), and an end to traditional Confucian values. It is often considered the birth of Chinese cultural modernism and had lasting effects on gender norms, literature, and politics.
📜
三从四德 (sāncóng sìdé) — "Three Obediences and Four Virtues" — the classical Confucian code of conduct for women. The Three Obediences: obey your father before marriage, obey your husband after marriage, obey your son after your husband dies. The Four Virtues: wifely virtue (德), appropriate speech (言), modest appearance (容), and diligent work (功).
🗣️
"多年的媳妇熬成婆" — a proverb meaning "After years of being a daughter-in-law, one finally becomes a mother-in-law." It describes a cycle of suffering: women who endured harsh treatment from their own mothers-in-law often repeated — or worsened — that treatment once they gained the same position of authority. The text uses this to argue that oppression can be self-perpetuating within a system.
📝 Vocabulary Note — 即使 vs. 甚至 / 权益 vs. 权利
⚖️
即使 (jíshǐ) = "even if / even though" — introduces a hypothetical or concessive condition. The outcome is true regardless. Structure: 即使…，也… ("Even if…, still…"). Compare: 即使没有被虐待，女性也受到压迫。 Do NOT confuse with 甚至 (which adds an extreme further point, not a condition).
⚖️
权益 (quányì) vs. 权利 (quánlì): Both relate to "rights," but 权利 refers to specific legal or civil rights (voting rights, property rights), while 权益 is broader — "rights and interests/benefits" — often used in contexts of protection from harm or exploitation (e.g., consumer rights, women's rights in a family context). Both appear in this text.

1949年以后，中国妇女的地位得到了很大的提高。她们开始在政治、经济、文化、教育等各个方面享有与男性平等的权利。她们不但有了婚姻自由，也跟男性一样有了选举权和被选举权。到七十年代末，中国有30%的政府官员是女性，这是中国几千年历史上从来没有过的现象。大多数女性走出家门，跟丈夫一样出去工作。这不但增加了家庭的收入，提高了生活水平，也改变了女性在社会和家庭中的地位。至此，中国基本上实现了男女平等。

🌏 Cultural Note — 1949 and the PRC
🏳️
1949 marks the founding of the People's Republic of China (中华人民共和国). The Chinese Communist Party promoted gender equality as a core ideological principle — Mao Zedong's slogan "Women hold up half the sky" (妇女能顶半边天) became nationally famous. The Marriage Law of 1950 explicitly banned foot-binding, child marriage, and concubinage, and gave women equal rights to divorce and inherit property.
🗳️
选举权和被选举权 — the right to vote and the right to stand for election. Chinese women gained both simultaneously with the 1949 Constitution — unlike in many Western democracies where these rights were granted separately and much later (e.g., women in Switzerland didn't gain full voting rights until 1971).
📝 Vocabulary Note — 享有 vs. 享受 / 至此
🔎
享有 (xiǎngyǒu) vs. 享受 (xiǎngshòu) — These look similar but are NOT interchangeable. 享有 is used for rights, status, or privileges: 享有选举权 (to enjoy voting rights), 享有声誉 (to have a good reputation). 享受 is for pleasures and material comforts: 享受美食 (to enjoy good food), 享受生活 (to enjoy life). Using 享受 for rights sounds odd in Chinese.
🔎
至此 (zhì cǐ) = "at this point; up to here; by this time." A formal written transition marker used to signal that a stage or process has been reached. Common in academic and journalistic writing. It signals a conclusion to a phase of the argument before moving on.

上个世纪八十年代前后，中国实行的改革开放政策使中国的经济得到了飞速发展。这期间，尤其是本世纪初以来，中国人的生活发生了翻天覆地的变化。过去的二十年间，中国为推进性别平等与妇女发展，制定了不少保护妇女权益的政策和法律。今天，接受高等教育的年轻女性越来越多，她们在毕业之后基本上都选择了进入职场。据调查，2013年，中国女性就业人数为三亿四千多万，是就业总人数的45%；15岁以上女性文盲率为6.7%，比1995年下降了17.4个百分点。另外，在个人收入方面，近些年中国女性的工资也明显提高，失业率连年下降。

🌏 Cultural Note — Reform Era & Statistics
📈
改革开放 (gǎigé kāifàng) — "Reform and Opening Up" — the economic policy initiated by Deng Xiaoping starting around 1978. It transformed China from a centrally planned economy to a market-oriented one, leading to decades of rapid GDP growth. For women, this brought expanded employment opportunities but also new pressures and inequalities as the market rewarded some forms of gender discrimination.
📊
The statistics cited (2013 data) reflect the period when this textbook was written. Note the essay's rhetorical strategy: it acknowledges genuine progress (employment numbers, literacy rates, wage increases) before pivoting to remaining problems in the next paragraph. This balanced structure — "progress, but…" — is a common pattern in analytical Chinese writing.
✏️ Grammar Note — 率 as a suffix / 高于 as a comparison
📐
率 (lǜ) — a suffix meaning "rate / ratio / percentage." It attaches to nouns to form compound words: 失业率 (unemployment rate), 文盲率 (illiteracy rate), 比率 (ratio), 出生率 (birth rate), 死亡率 (death/mortality rate). It always comes at the end.

以上成就值得肯定，但我们也要认识到，要从根本上实现男女性别平等，我们还有很大的努力空间。目前，男性的平均收入仍然远高于女性。根据2011年全球性别差距报告，中国的男女收入比率为1: 0.65，这与世界发达国家相比还有很大的差别。另外，女性中"干得好不如嫁得好"的观念重新抬头，有些地方"包二奶"的现象普遍，妇女的地位甚至有走下坡路的危险。

🌏 Cultural Note — Contemporary Challenges
💬
"干得好不如嫁得好" — "It's better to marry well than to work well." This popular saying reflects a cultural backlash against women's professional advancement in modern China. With rising property prices and wealth inequality, some young women began to see marrying a wealthy man as more economically rational than building a career — a trend that concerned feminist scholars and the government alike.
💰
包二奶 (bāo èrnǎi) — literally "support a second wife/mistress." This phenomenon became more visible during China's economic boom of the 1990s–2000s, when newly wealthy businessmen in some regions kept mistresses (often called 二奶, "second wives"), housed and financially supported by them. It is widely considered exploitative and a marker of gender inequality.
✏️ Grammar Note — 高于 / 远高于 as comparisons
📐
高于 (gāo yú) = "higher than" — an alternative comparison structure to 比. With 于: 男性收入高于女性 (Men's income is higher than women's). With 比: 男性收入比女性高 (same meaning). The 于-structure is more formal and common in written/analytical texts. Other examples: 低于 (lower than), 多于 (more than), 少于 (fewer than), 大于 (greater than).
📐
远高于 — "far/much higher than." Adding 远 (far) before a comparative adjective intensifies it: 远高于, 远低于, 远大于. This is a common pattern in formal Chinese writing when making strong contrasts.

总之，过去的几十年中，中国在保护妇女、提高妇女地位问题上做出了很多努力。但作为世界上人口最多的发展中国家，受经济发展水平和传统文化的影响，中国妇女的地位问题还面临很多新的挑战。因此，要实现女性跟男性的真正平等，除了政府的政策及相应的法律之外，还需要女性提高自身的素质，努力创造一个自由、平等、无性别歧视的社会环境。我们期待着中国女性在不久的将来拥有一个更广阔的天空。

✏️ Grammar Note — Conclusion Structures / 除了…之外，还…
📐
除了…之外，还需要… — "In addition to…, one also needs…" This structure adds a further requirement or condition. It's common in analytical conclusions: 除了政府的政策之外，还需要个人努力。 (In addition to government policy, individual effort is also needed.) Do not confuse this with the exclusive 除了…以外 (除了他，大家都来了 — "Everyone came except him").
📐
总之 (zǒng zhī) = "in short; in a word; in conclusion." A common discourse marker signaling a summary or concluding statement. Similar transitions: 综上所述 (more formal, "based on the above"), 因此 (therefore), 所以 (so).
💡
素质 (sùzhì) — "quality; overall character and capability." Often translated as "quality" but refers to a person's or group's inner moral, intellectual, and cultural development. "提高素质" is a frequent phrase in Chinese public discourse about social improvement — it implies that individuals should cultivate themselves, not just rely on external systems.
