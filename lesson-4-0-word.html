Focus on Patterns

Learn through radicals, semantic fields, and character families

1. 女字旁：很多和女性身份、婚姻、家庭有关

This lesson is especially good for teaching 女字旁. Many characters with 女 relate to women, marriage, kinship, or historically gendered social roles.

妇fùwoman / married woman
妇女fùnǚwomen
媳xídaughter-in-law
媳妇xífùwife / daughter-in-law
婆pómother-in-law
公婆gōngpóparents-in-law
奴núslave
奴隶núlìslave
Memory tip: 女字旁 often becomes a useful visual entry into words about women’s identity, household roles, and historically gendered expectations. In this lesson, 妇女 / 媳妇 / 公婆 / 奴隶 form a very teachable cluster.

2. 和“女”相关的词：不一定都带女字旁

A second important teaching move is to show students that not every women-related word must contain 女. Some key ideas belong to the same semantic field even without the radical.

女性nǚxìngwoman, female
性别xìngbiégender, sex
婚姻hūnyīnmarriage
夫权fūquánhusband’s authority
平等píngděngequal
职场zhíchǎngworkplace
Memory tip: teach students to build a meaning field, not only a radical family. A word can belong to the topic of women’s status even if it does not visually contain 女. That is why 性别 / 婚姻 / 平等 / 职场 are important to group together.
Why is it useful to group 性别, 婚姻, 平等, and 职场 with 女-related vocabulary?

3. 米字旁：从“糟糕”进去记“食物、质地、状态”

This lesson does not have many 米-radical words, but it gives one very useful anchor: 糕 in 糟糕. From there, students can expand toward food, grain, or rough / sticky material associations in other familiar words.

糕gāocake
糟糕zāogāoterrible
米mǐrice
粮liánggrain
粉fěnpowder
粗cūcoarse
Memory tip: here 米字旁 is best treated as an extension pattern. Start from 糟糕, then tell students: “This radical often appears in words related to grain, food texture, powder, or material feel.” Even when the modern meaning has shifted, the visual family still helps memory.

4. 木字旁：从“权”走进权利、权益、夫权

This lesson is actually very good for teaching 木字旁 through the simplified character 权. Once students notice that 权 begins with 木, they can build a very useful modern concept network.

权quánpower / right
夫权fūquánhusband’s authority
权益quányìrights and benefits
权利quánlìrights
女权nǚquánwomen’s rights
人权rénquánhuman rights
Memory tip: this is a powerful example of using a single character family to organize abstract social vocabulary. Start from 权, then expand to 夫权 / 权益 / 权利, and then optionally to 女权 / 人权. This gives students a clear conceptual map, not just isolated words.

Usage Guide · 用法辨析

Words students commonly confuse or misuse

These words appear in Lesson 4.0 and are frequently misused by English-speaking learners — either because of a misleading English translation or because they look or sound similar to another word.

1
见不得 VS 见不得人
Two different phrases — not the same!
见不得 (jiàn bu dé) = "cannot stand to see; cannot tolerate the sight of." Used when you dislike or are disturbed by something.

见不得人 (jiànbudé rén) = "shameful; not fit to be seen by others; embarrassing." Describes something morally improper or disgraceful — it has nothing to do with literally "seeing people."
✓ 她见不得别人受苦。
She cannot stand to see others suffer.
✓ 宋朝以前，再嫁并不是什么见不得人的事。
Before the Song Dynasty, remarrying was not a shameful thing.
✗ 她做了见不得的事。
❌ Incomplete — must say 见不得人 (shameful deed).
2
享有 VS 享受
Both mean "enjoy" — but for different objects
享有 (xiǎngyǒu) = "to enjoy/hold (a right, status, or privilege)." Used for abstract entitlements: rights, reputation, authority, equal treatment.

享受 (xiǎngshòu) = "to enjoy (a pleasure or comfort)." Used for concrete, sensory, or material things: food, life, vacation, music. The two are NOT interchangeable.
✓ 她们开始享有与男性平等的权利。
They began to enjoy equal rights with men.
✓ 她享受着假期的宁静。
She is enjoying the peace of the vacation.
✗ 她们开始享受平等的权利。
❌ 享受 sounds unnatural with abstract rights.
3
甚至 VS 即使
Both involve "even" — but very different structures
甚至 (shènzhì) = "even; so much so that" — adds an extreme, surprising further point in a statement. No condition involved.

即使…也… (jíshǐ…yě…) = "even if / even though…, still…" — introduces a hypothetical or concessive condition. Always paired with 也. The outcome holds regardless.
✓ 她找不到婆家，甚至被人耻笑。
She couldn't find a husband's family, and was even mocked.
✓ 即使没有受到虐待，女性也得受到压迫。
Even if not abused, women still faced oppression.
✗ 即使她找不到婆家，被人耻笑。
❌ 即使 requires 也 in the second clause.
4
权利 VS 权益
Similar meaning, different scope
权利 (quánlì) = specific legal or civil rights — voting rights, property rights, the right to an education. Often appears in legal or constitutional contexts.

权益 (quányì) = "rights and interests" — a broader term covering both rights and the benefits or well-being attached to them. Often used when talking about protection from harm, exploitation, or unfair treatment.
✓ 她们享有选举权利。
They enjoy the right to vote.
✓ 许多女性的家庭权益受到侵犯。
Many women's family rights and interests were violated.
💡 保护妇女权益 (protect women's rights and interests) is the set phrase used in Chinese law and policy — not 权利 alone.
5
虐待 VS 歧视
Abuse vs. discrimination — not interchangeable
虐待 (nüèdài) = "to abuse; to mistreat; to subject to cruel treatment" — physical or emotional harm inflicted on a person. Active, direct harm.

歧视 (qíshì) = "to discriminate against; to treat with prejudice" — treating someone unfairly based on their identity (gender, race, etc.). More systemic, attitudinal.
✓ 后母虐待女儿的情况经常发生。
Cases of stepmothers abusing their daughters happened often.
✓ 中国历史上歧视妇女的现象很多。
There were many instances of discrimination against women in Chinese history.
✗ 中国历史上虐待妇女的现象很多。
❌ Possible but too strong for a general opening — 歧视 fits better here.
6
至此
A formal written transition — not everyday speech
至此 (zhì cǐ) = "at this point; up to here; by now; thus far" — a formal written transition marker signaling that a described process has reached a stage or conclusion. It is almost exclusively used in written Chinese and would sound unusual in speech. Common alternatives in speech: 到这里, 就这样, 这样一来.
✎ Written 至此，中国基本上实现了男女平等。
At this point, China had essentially achieved gender equality.
💡 至此 is often followed by a summary statement. It marks a milestone in a narrative or argument, similar to "thus" or "by this point" in English academic writing.
✗ 至此，我们去吃饭吧。
❌ Too casual — 至此 does not fit informal spoken contexts.
7
高于 / 低于 / 多于
The 于-comparison structure in formal writing
Adjective + 于 + noun = a formal comparison structure used in written Chinese, especially in reports and academic texts. It replaces the conversational 比 structure.

高于 (higher than)　· 　低于 (lower than)
多于 (more than)　 · 　少于 (fewer than)
大于 (greater than) · 　小于 (less than)

Adding 远 (far/much) intensifies: 远高于, 远低于, 远多于.
✎ Written 男性的平均收入仍然远高于女性。
Men's average income remains far higher than women's.
💡 Spoken equivalent: 男性的平均收入比女性高得多。
(Same meaning, more conversational.)
